Tweet / Threads Character Splitter

Paste your long-form text below and it'll be automatically broken into numbered posts that fit within X (Twitter) or Threads character limits — splitting at sentence or word boundaries, never mid-word.

0 characters

Why split text at sentence boundaries instead of hard character cutoffs?

A naive splitter that just cuts text every 280 characters will frequently break a post in the middle of a word or sentence, which reads awkwardly and looks unpolished to your audience. This tool instead looks for natural breakpoints — sentence endings, then word boundaries as a fallback — so each post in the thread reads as a complete thought, even though it's part of a longer series.

Numbering each post (like "1/5") also matters for readability, especially on platforms where threads can get interrupted by replies from other people. A clear numbering system helps readers know where they are in your thread and whether they've seen the whole thing, which in turn tends to improve completion rates on longer threads.

Does the character count include the "1/5" numbering suffix?

Yes — the tool reserves space for the numbering suffix so your final post text plus the counter never exceeds the platform's limit.

Can I edit individual posts after splitting?

Currently each post is generated as read-only text you can copy individually. Edit your source text and re-split if you need changes.
