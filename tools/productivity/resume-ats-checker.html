How ATS software actually screens resumes

Applicant Tracking Systems parse your resume into plain text before a recruiter ever sees it, which means formatting choices that look great visually — tables, text boxes, headers/footers, columns, or images — can scramble or completely drop content during parsing. A resume that looks perfect when you open it in Word can arrive at the recruiter's screen missing your entire work history if it was built with the wrong layout choices.

Beyond formatting, most ATS platforms also rank or filter candidates based on keyword overlap with the job description — skills, tools, and job titles mentioned in the posting. This doesn't mean stuffing keywords unnaturally; it means making sure the specific terms a recruiter searched for (like "React" or "project management") actually appear in your resume if they genuinely describe your experience, rather than being implied through different wording.

Does this tool read my actual resume file (PDF/Word)?

No — paste the text content directly. This avoids parsing issues and keeps the check focused purely on your resume's wording and structure, which is what matters for ATS scoring.

Is a high ATS score a guarantee of getting an interview?

No — ATS compatibility just ensures your resume is readable and relevant to the role at the screening stage. The actual hiring decision still depends on your experience, the interview, and factors this tool can't measure.
