Why should you convert images to WebP?

WebP is a modern image format developed by Google that typically produces files 25-35% smaller than equivalent JPEGs at the same visual quality, and even more compared to PNG for images with transparency. Since image weight is usually the single biggest contributor to page load time, converting your website's images to WebP is one of the most effective website speed optimization changes you can make without touching any other part of your site.

This matters beyond just user experience — page speed is a confirmed ranking factor for search engines, and Google's own PageSpeed Insights and Lighthouse tools specifically flag unoptimized images and recommend switching to next-gen image formats like WebP or AVIF. AVIF compresses even further than WebP in many cases, though browser support is still catching up, which is why offering both formats gives you flexibility depending on your audience's browsers.

Is WebP better than JPG?

For most use cases, yes — WebP produces smaller file sizes at comparable visual quality, and unlike JPG, it also supports transparency, so it can replace PNG in many situations too. The main caveat is very old browsers or software with no WebP support, though this is increasingly rare today.

Does WebP reduce image quality?

At high quality settings (80% and above), the visual difference from the original is generally imperceptible, while the file size reduction is still substantial. Lower quality settings save more space but introduce visible compression artifacts, similar to how JPEG quality settings work.
