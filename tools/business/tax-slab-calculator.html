New vs Old Tax Regime — which should you choose?

The new tax regime offers lower slab rates and a higher rebate threshold — income up to ₹12 lakh is effectively tax-free after the Section 87A rebate — but it removes most deductions like 80C investments, HRA, and home loan interest. The old regime keeps higher slab rates but lets you reduce your taxable income significantly if you have substantial deductions to claim, such as ELSS investments, life insurance premiums, or rent paid.

As a rough rule of thumb, if your eligible deductions under the old regime add up to more than roughly ₹4-5 lakh (varies by income level), the old regime often comes out ahead. Below that, the new regime's lower rates and higher rebate usually win. Since the actual break-even point shifts depending on your income level, running both calculations side by side — like this tool does — is the most reliable way to decide rather than relying on a fixed rule.

Can I switch between regimes every year?

Salaried individuals can choose either regime each financial year when filing returns. Those with business income have more restrictions on switching back and forth — confirm your specific situation with a tax professional.

Is this calculation accurate enough to file my taxes?

This tool estimates tax liability based on standard slabs and rebates for FY 2026-27, but doesn't account for every possible deduction, surcharge edge case, or marginal relief scenario. Use it for planning, and confirm final figures with a tax professional or the official income tax portal before filing.
