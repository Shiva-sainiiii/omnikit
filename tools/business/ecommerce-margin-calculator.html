Why RTO destroys e-commerce margins more than sellers expect

A product that sells for ₹999 with a cost of ₹350 looks like it has a healthy margin — until RTO enters the picture. When an order is returned to origin, the seller typically eats both the forward shipping cost (to send it out) and the reverse shipping cost (to bring it back), while earning zero revenue from that order. If your RTO rate is 15%, that means roughly one in seven orders is a pure loss, and this loss has to be absorbed by the profit from your successfully delivered orders — which quietly shrinks your real margin far more than most sellers realize when they're only looking at price minus cost.

This is especially significant for cash-on-delivery (COD) heavy categories in India, where RTO rates are often much higher than prepaid orders, since customers can refuse delivery without any financial commitment upfront. Calculating true net profit — factoring in platform commission, payment gateway fees, and RTO losses averaged across your order volume — gives a far more accurate picture than the naive "selling price minus cost price" math most new sellers start with.

How can I reduce my RTO rate?

Common approaches include offering prepaid discounts to shift customers away from COD, adding order confirmation calls or SMS verification, and improving product page accuracy to reduce refusals from mismatched expectations. Results vary by category and audience.

Does this include GST or income tax on my profit?

No — this calculates operational net profit before tax. Use the Tax Slab Calculator separately to estimate income tax on your business earnings.
